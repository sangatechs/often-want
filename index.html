Why the Hijri Gap Differs from the Gregorian Gap

The Hijri year runs approximately 11 days shorter than the Gregorian year. This means that when you calculate the age gap between a parent and child in Hijri terms, the result will be slightly larger than the Gregorian gap — by an amount that grows with the size of the gap itself.

For a 25-year Gregorian gap, the Hijri equivalent is approximately 25 years and 9 months. For a 30-year Gregorian gap, the Hijri equivalent is roughly 30 years and 11 months. The larger the generational distance, the more the two systems diverge.

In countries where official family records use Hijri dates, the father's age and the child's age on those documents will show a Hijri gap. When those same people interact with international systems — embassies, universities, hospitals — the gap converts to Gregorian. Having both numbers ready prevents confusion and documentation errors.
